IBM Stock Analysis (ARIMA + GARCH, 1967–Present)

1. Introduction

This report presents a comprehensive time series analysis of IBM stock prices from January 1, 1967, to the present. The analysis employs a combination of Autoregressive Integrated Moving Average (ARIMA) for modeling the mean component of the time series and Generalized Autoregressive Conditional Heteroskedasticity (GARCH) for modeling the volatility component.

The primary objectives of this analysis are:

  • To understand the historical price patterns and statistical properties of IBM stock
  • To develop and evaluate a forecasting model that captures both trend and volatility
  • To provide insights for investment decision-making based on the model's performance

10. Recommendations and Insights

Model Suitability

The ARIMA(1,1,1) + GARCH(1,1) model provides a reasonable baseline for IBM stock price forecasting. The model captures both trend components through ARIMA and volatility components through GARCH. The performance metrics indicate limited forecasting accuracy with a MAPE of 21.63%.

Limitations

  • The linear nature of ARIMA may not capture complex market dynamics and non-linear patterns
  • GARCH assumes symmetric response to positive and negative shocks, which may not reflect market reality
  • The model may not adequately respond to sudden market shifts or black swan events
  • Long-term forecasts become increasingly uncertain due to error accumulation

Advanced Model Considerations

Based on the analysis, we recommend considering the following advanced models for potentially improved forecasting:

  • Silverkite: Better for capturing multiple seasonalities and holiday effects in financial time series
  • Orbit: Bayesian approach provides more robust uncertainty quantification and handles regime changes
  • TimesFM: Foundation models may capture complex non-linear patterns and incorporate external factors

Practical Applications

The current ARIMA+GARCH model is most suitable for:

  • Short-term forecasting (1-3 months ahead)
  • Volatility estimation for risk management
  • Complementing fundamental analysis for investment decisions
  • Establishing a baseline for comparison with more sophisticated models

For improved accuracy, consider ensemble approaches that combine multiple forecasting methods or incorporating external factors such as market sentiment, macroeconomic indicators, and company-specific news.
